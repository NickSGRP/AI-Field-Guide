Most AI projects don't fail because the tech doesn't work. They fail because the build started before the thinking did. D.R.A.F.T. is a worksheet — pick the option that fits, then write a sentence or two about your specific case. At the end, you'll see how complex your build actually is, who you'll need to make it happen, and how to scope it down (or up) until it feels right.
First, name your project
DQuestion 1 of 5 · Destination
Destination
Who is this AI tool actually for?
The audience determines almost everything else — security, support, training, and how much it has to be "right" the first time. Be honest about the most ambitious user, not the easiest one.
Pick the closest fit
In your own words
Who specifically? What do they do? Why this group?
This is your project, in your words. The clearer here, the sharper your final result.
RQuestion 2 of 5 · Records
Records
What kind of data will it touch?
This decides whether you can use a consumer tool or need an enterprise contract — and whether APES 110, the Privacy Act, and your engagement letters apply. Choose the most sensitive data type it will ever see.
Pick the most sensitive data type
In your own words
What data exactly? Where does it live today? Who currently has access?
Be specific. "Client data" is too broad — name the actual records.
AQuestion 3 of 5 · Autonomy
Autonomy
How much should it decide on its own?
The line between an assistant and an agent. Higher autonomy means faster work — and more review burden, more liability, and harder explanations to clients and regulators when something goes wrong.
Pick the level of trust
In your own words
What should it do, and what should always be a human's call?
The clearer the line between AI and human, the easier it is to defend later.
FQuestion 4 of 5 · Fit
Fit
How does this sit alongside your existing systems and processes?
How connected the tool is to your existing systems and processes. A separate tool is a weekend project. An embedded one is a year-long program. Each connection is a dependency you'll need to maintain.
Pick the level of integration
In your own words
Which systems does it need to talk to? Where does the input come from, and where does the output go?
Each system you name is a dependency you'll need to maintain.
TQuestion 5 of 5 · Transfer
Transfer
Who do you transfer ownership and responsibility to?
The question almost everyone forgets. Building it is one thing — handing it over is another. AI tools rot: models change, prompts drift, context shifts, people leave. Decide upfront who takes it on, or be honest that no one will.
Pick the transfer posture
In your own words
Who specifically takes it on? What does the hand-off actually look like?
Vague ownership is no ownership. Name a person or accept it's an experiment.
